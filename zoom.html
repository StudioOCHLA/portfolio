Zoom Case Study

Feature design with the user in mind

The objective is to modify the user interface of a digital artefact through a user-centered design approach, thereby improving its adaptability to users and their contexts. My research led to three design proposals…. CONTINUE…

Problem

Work-from-home user

During quarantine, people are looking for ways to effectively interact virtually and adapt to work-from-home lifestyles. They are becoming reliant on video chatting platforms, such as Zoom, to communicate and run their businesses. With changing user habits, how can we improve this product to make the work-from-home experience easier and more efficient?

Three design concepts were created for an improved user interface, particularly regarding screen sharing. The project was part of a university interaction design course i stood for all the work.

Solution

In-call feature

The goal is to improve the user interface, particularly in relation to screen sharing.

I have developed three new, different, and unique user interface suggestions for the artefact based on the user's context and use. The design of each interface proposal has been based on different design principles, as per Donald Norman (2013: The Design of Everyday Things).

CHANGE TO MY TEXT. The in-call meetings feature helps users quickly navigate between meetings and access their schedules and information while in ongoing calls. Information and actions are consolidated into a concise and accessible window to make the process of organizing and attending virtual meetings simpler and faster.

Context of use

Professional contexts include the use of the artefact, such as internal and external corporate meetings. Online seminars can be created, scheduled, and participated in by the user both during and before these meetings.

Digital channels are used to send the invitation, either through a hyperlink or by the user entering a 'meeting ID'. The user has the option to share their screen and webcam with other participants if desired.

General findings from analyses

  • CHANGE. Platforms should include safety measures (password, controls, etc).
  • CHANGE. Platforms should be able to accommodate at least 100-150 people.
  • CHANGE. Virtual backgrounds are a good feature for those with distracting backgrounds.
  • CHANGE. Waiting rooms are helpful to let you know who you are letting in.
  • CHANGE. Dashboards are helpful for planning calls.

Interview

I have conducted interviews with individuals to gain more user perspective about their video chat habits, most-used platforms, and issues with existing products. Zoom being one of the most frequently used products led me to prioritize its product improvement.

Survey

General Interviews - I asked participants about their habits, commonly used platforms and video chat pain points.

Insights based on the survey

The primary use of Zoom is for work. Users are accustomed to use of the artefact and appreciates the screen-sharing feature. Most pain points relate to unexpected interruptions and delays, which can cause irritation and stress.

Personas

At a reputable game development company in Malmo, a 30-year-old employee in UI and UX design is attending several daily meetings, mainly following pre-set schedules.

Ideation

Having a better understanding of existing pain points and user contexts, I brainstormed on features and potential enhancements. I employed the double diamond design process model, which is a user-centred design process, to prioritize ideas and determine which ones were more practical.

The double diamond design process model.

Conclusion based on the design process model

I decided to focus on improving 'discoverability' and 'constraints'. My ideas were most concentrated in that category and more practical (high value and low cost = CHANGE).

High Fidelity Prototype 1

Constraints

The menu selection in this design proposal is limited to only possible actions through constraints and signifiers.

As delimitation reduces the amount of interaction possible and the information that the user needs to keep in memory, it is easier for the user to use the artefact.

High Fidelity Prototype 2

Conceptual model

A good conceptual model enables the user to comprehend the artefact's structure and the actions they can take with it.

Feedback confirms the current status of the artefact in each performed action, which helps clarify and improve the design. The mapping of the artefact makes it even more easy to use, as the possible actions are clarified at present.

High Fidelity Prototype 3

Discoverability

This design proposal makes it easy to comprehend all possible actions through perceived affordances and descriptive signifiers.

In my view, these signifiers drive the primary goal of the interface proposal, which is to improve its discoverability. The center object is highlighted and explained, and it is clearly configured.
